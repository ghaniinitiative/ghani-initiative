Target area — Canopy

The largest tree plantation NGO in Sheikhupura. Said plainly, on purpose.

Most organizations hedge their ambitions. We put ours on a tracker at the top of the page. Native neem, sheesham, jamun, and amaltas along canal banks, schoolyards, graveyards, and roadside verges — chosen for survival in this soil, not for how they photograph. Planting day is the easy part; our crews return at 30, 90, and 365 days, because a tree only joins the counter once it has survived a Punjab summer.

In practice → survival-audited groves, publicly pinned, one district made measurably greener.

Target area — Engines

We don't hand out relief. We hand over engines.

A ration bag runs out by month's end and the queue re-forms. So we stopped funding queues. Instead: a fully equipped mobile fruit cart — the cart itself, a scale, the first day's stock, a route walked together, help with the vendor paperwork. The person who stood in an aid line on Monday is a business owner by Friday, with prices they set and takings that are entirely theirs. No repayment schedule. The only ask: when the engine runs strong, help us seed the next one.

In practice → a funded cart typically clears its own cost within a season. Then it's simply theirs.

Target area — Craft

The Bazaar isn't charity. It's a premium pipeline.

Generations of weaving, throwing, carving, and silverwork deserve better than a pity purchase. Our digital Bazaar routes that mastery straight to buyers at full global market value — never "local rates." Creator autonomy is absolute: the maker sets the price, keeps their name on the work, and is paid first. Every remaining rupee of margin cycles back into the foundation. Heritage, honored commercially.

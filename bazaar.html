This is not
a charity shop.
It's a hall of masters.

Every piece below left the hands of a named artisan from the villages around Sheikhupura. Nobody here is asking for your sympathy — they're offering you work that took decades to master, priced at full global value, never "local rates."

Where every rupee goes

You request a piece

Straight to a human logistics desk — no cart, no checkout maze.

The maker is paid first

At maximum global market value — their price, their name, their signature.

Margins fund the movement

Every remaining rupee flows back into self-reliance infrastructure. We keep nothing.

Why is there no cart?

Because a cart can't answer questions, and a checkout page has never met a maker. Every dispatch request lands with a real person at our logistics desk in Sheikhupura — usually a volunteer sitting a few kilometres from the workshop itself. They confirm the piece, the timeline, and the delivery, human to human. Slower than an algorithm. Better in every other way.

The point of all this

Every piece you request is a vote for someone's independence.

And if you'd rather give hours than rupees — the movement always has room for one more pair of hands.

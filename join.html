The people running this — pinning groves across Sheikhupura, rolling out fruit carts, packing artisan orders for the Bazaar — are not specialists, celebrities, or career philanthropists. They're a schoolteacher, a shopkeeper, a couple of students, a retired clerk. Ordinary people. Exactly like you.

None of them filled out a grant application or waited for an agency to arrive. They saw a bare canal bank, an empty aid queue, a craft dying quietly — and simply started. The tools were borrowed. The plan fit on one page. It was enough.

The momentum is already moving. The only thing between you and it is the form below — and it's shorter than this paragraph.

Field story — Canopy Crew

When the water bowser broke in June, Iqra didn't file a complaint. She got on her bike.

Nine kilometres each way, every day, through a 47° heatwave — hand-watering three hundred saplings on the Farooqabad stretch from canisters strapped to her carrier. When the audit crew returned at day ninety, her section logged the best survival rate in the district. She still insists it "wasn't a big deal." The trees disagree.

Iqra Shahid · Survival Audits, since 2024

Field story — Enterprise Support

Cart #1 was built in Usman's courtyard. Its axle broke in a week. What he did next became the blueprint.

Village roads eat standard axles for breakfast. Instead of buying another, Usman spent three consecutive weekends redesigning the undercarriage — wider stance, reinforced welds, salvaged motorcycle bearings. Every one of the sixteen carts built since rolls on his design, and he inspects each new build personally before it's handed over.

Usman Ghani · Cart Assembly, since 2024

Field story — Canopy Crew

Rabia walked into six headmasters' offices with one map and walked out with six schoolyard groves.

Her pitch took four minutes: shade for assemblies, fruit for the canteen, a science lesson growing outside the window. Every school said yes. Then she went further — organizing students into "grove guardian" rotas, so each tree has a child who knows it by name. Those groves now have the highest survival rates of any site type we plant.

Rabia Noor · School Groves, since 2025
